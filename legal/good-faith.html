Good Faith Statement

Published: April 14, 2026 · Version 1.0

A public statement from BurgTV on copyright, user responsibility, piracy, and our commitment to legitimate use of the internet.

TL;DR: BurgTV is a neutral media player. We do not endorse, facilitate, or profit from piracy. We respect copyright law, cooperate with rights holders, and build our app to serve legitimate media consumption.

1. What BurgTV Is

BurgTV is a media player. Technically, legally, and functionally, it belongs to the same category as VLC Media Player, MX Player, Kodi, Plex client, and Jellyfin client. Like those applications, BurgTV is a neutral tool: it plays the media you give it, and nothing more.

BurgTV does not:

  • Host any media content on its servers
  • Provide, curate, or recommend streaming sources
  • Include pre-loaded playlists, channels, or URLs
  • Aggregate content from third-party streaming services
  • Embed or facilitate access to piracy websites
  • Direct users toward unauthorized content

2. What BurgTV Is For

We built BurgTV for users who have legitimate media sources and need a high-quality player to consume them:

  • Users with paid IPTV subscriptions from legitimate licensed providers (think of services that legally distribute TV channels across countries where they hold broadcast rights)
  • Self-hosters running their own Plex, Jellyfin, or Emby servers, wanting a capable client on their TV
  • Content creators wanting to test their own M3U streams on real devices
  • Enterprises and broadcasters using BurgTV for internal distribution or demo purposes
  • Users of public-domain or Creative Commons content wanting a unified playback tool

3. Our Position on Piracy

We do not support, tolerate, or facilitate piracy in any form.

Using BurgTV to access unauthorized, pirated, or illegally-redistributed content is a violation of our Terms of Service and, in most jurisdictions, a violation of copyright law.

We recognize that, like any general-purpose tool (a web browser, a VCR, a photocopier, an iPhone), BurgTV could theoretically be misused. This is true of every piece of software in the media space, including products from major tech companies. Courts have long recognized that the creator of a neutral tool is not legally responsible for users who misuse it (see Sony Corp. of America v. Universal City Studios, 464 U.S. 417, also known as the "Betamax case").

Still, we take responsibility for making our position unambiguous:

  • Our Terms of Service explicitly prohibit using BurgTV for illegal content
  • Our Acceptable Use Policy lists specific prohibited activities
  • Our DMCA Policy provides clear takedown procedures
  • The app shows an explicit disclaimer about content sources
  • We do not advertise or market BurgTV as a tool for pirated content
  • We do not partner with illegal streaming services

4. Why Users Are Responsible for Their Content

Like a car manufacturer is not responsible for drivers who speed, and a hammer maker is not responsible for violence, a media-player developer cannot be held responsible for every use of their software.

"The sale of copying equipment, like the sale of other articles of commerce, does not constitute contributory infringement if the product is widely used for legitimate, unobjectionable purposes."
— Sony Corp. v. Universal, 464 U.S. 417 (1984)

This principle — often called the "Sony Betamax doctrine" in the US and echoed in EU case law (e.g., GS Media v. Sanoma, C-160/15) — is the legal foundation for every neutral technology, from the VCR to the iPhone. BurgTV operates entirely within this framework.

The responsibility for what is played rests with the user who chose and added the source. BurgTV provides the playback capability; the user provides the content.

5. Our Cooperative Commitments

We cooperate with rights holders in good faith.

BurgTV maintains a dedicated DMCA channel, a transparency report, and a rapid response team for copyright issues.

Specifically, BurgTV commits to:

  • Respond to valid DMCA notices within 48 business hours
  • Publish a quarterly Transparency Report documenting all DMCA activity
  • Cooperate with valid legal process from law enforcement (within the limits of data we actually possess)
  • Immediately remove any feature demonstrably facilitating piracy if such a feature were identified
  • Update our Terms of Service as needed to reflect evolving legal standards
  • Participate in industry dialogue with rights holders and regulators

6. On App Stores and Content Policy

We recognize that app stores (Amazon Appstore, Google Play, Apple App Store) apply their own content policies which can be stricter than the law itself. Apps in the "IPTV player" category are frequently flagged by automated systems, regardless of legal compliance.

BurgTV's approach to this:

  • We engage constructively with app store reviewers when flagged
  • We provide this Good Faith Statement and our full legal documentation to support our position
  • When a store declines, we respect the decision and seek alternative distribution (direct download, alternative stores) rather than misrepresenting our app
  • We do not submit repeatedly with modifications designed to deceive review systems

The fact that we operate outside some major stores is not an admission of wrongdoing — it is a consequence of overly-cautious automated content policies. Many legitimate apps (Kodi, VLC at one point, Tor Browser on some stores) have faced similar classifications.

7. What We Ask of Users

We ask users to:

  • Use BurgTV with legitimate sources only — your own paid subscription, your own server, public-domain content, or legally-licensed streams
  • Respect copyright law in your jurisdiction
  • Report abuse you observe via abuse@burgtv.com
  • Read our Terms of Service before signing up for Premium

8. A Note to Rights Holders

If you are a rights holder and believe your content is being accessed through BurgTV without authorization, we want to help. Please:

  • Understand that BurgTV does not host, curate, or direct users to infringing content
  • Note that the relevant target for enforcement is usually the source of the content (the IPTV provider, the website, the server), not the player
  • Use our DMCA process at dmca@burgtv.com for content on our servers (there is none, but we'll clarify)
  • Contact us at legal@burgtv.com for cooperative discussions

9. Closing Note

We built BurgTV because we wanted a better media player — one that's fast, elegant, privacy-respecting, and works well on our TVs. We believe that neutral tools that empower users are good for the internet, and that the benefits of such tools vastly outweigh potential misuse by a minority.

We commit to operating BurgTV in good faith, in compliance with applicable law, and with respect for the legitimate interests of rights holders, users, regulators, and the broader ecosystem.

— The BurgTV Team Switzerland · April 14, 2026

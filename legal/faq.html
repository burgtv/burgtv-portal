Legal FAQ

Last updated: April 14, 2026

Answers to the most common legal questions about BurgTV. If you need clarification beyond what is covered here, contact legal@burgtv.com.

Is BurgTV legal?

Yes. BurgTV is a neutral media player, legally equivalent to well-known media players such as VLC Media Player, MX Player, Kodi, or Plex. The app itself:

  • Does not host, distribute, stream, or provide any copyrighted content
  • Does not include any pre-loaded playlists, channels, or streaming URLs
  • Does not promote or link to piracy websites, torrents, or illegal sources
  • Requires users to provide their own legitimate media source

The legality of the content you play through BurgTV depends entirely on whether your source is legitimate. BurgTV is not responsible for user content.

What is a "neutral media player"?

A neutral media player is software designed solely to play back media files or streams, without itself providing, indexing, or aggregating content. The player is content-agnostic: it plays what you give it.

Examples of widely accepted neutral media players: VLC, MX Player, mpv, Kodi (core), ExoPlayer, Plex client, Jellyfin client.

BurgTV falls into the same category. The media you play is entirely under your control and sourced from your own legitimate subscription or server.

Can I use BurgTV with pirated IPTV subscriptions?

No. Using BurgTV to access unauthorized, pirated, or illegally-redistributed streams is a violation of our Terms of Service and Acceptable Use Policy.

BurgTV is designed for use with:

  • Legitimate IPTV subscriptions purchased from authorized providers
  • Your own Plex, Jellyfin, Emby, or similar self-hosted media server
  • Creative Commons or public-domain content you own the rights to
  • Legal M3U playlists from broadcasters who explicitly authorize redistribution
Who is legally responsible for what I watch through BurgTV?

You are. BurgTV is a passive tool, like a web browser or a video player. The responsibility for the legality of content accessed rests entirely with the user (you).

Courts worldwide have consistently held that neutral tools (VCR, web browsers, media players) are not liable for user misuse. This doctrine (the "Sony Betamax doctrine" in the USA, and similar principles in EU case law) is the basis on which BurgTV operates.

What if I accidentally use a pirated source?

If you unknowingly added a pirated IPTV source, remove it immediately from Settings → Playlists. No logs are kept on our servers of what you watch.

If you receive a notice from your ISP or a rights holder, the responsibility is yours. BurgTV cannot protect users from the legal consequences of accessing unauthorized content.

Does BurgTV comply with DMCA?

Yes. BurgTV complies fully with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA, USA), the EU Copyright Directive (2001/29/EC and 2019/790), and similar national copyright frameworks.

We maintain a full DMCA Takedown Policy with dedicated contact procedures at dmca@burgtv.com.

We respond to valid takedown notices within 48 hours (business days).

Yes. BurgTV is compliant with:

Why is BurgTV not on Google Play / App Store / Amazon?

Many official app stores apply stricter content policies than the law itself. Apps classified as "IPTV players" are frequently rejected by automated review systems, even when the app is legally a neutral media player.

BurgTV has been submitted to multiple stores. We're available on APKPure, Huawei AppGallery, and via direct download from download.burgtv.com. We continue to work with store reviews to clarify our neutral-player nature.

This situation is common in our category — similar apps like IPTV Smarters, Kodi, and Xciptv are distributed primarily outside main stores.

Can I distribute BurgTV or the APK to others?

Yes, you can freely share the original, unmodified APK downloaded from download.burgtv.com or our official GitHub releases. The app is free for personal use.

You may not:

  • Modify or repackage the APK (this breaks the digital signature)
  • Distribute the app bundled with pirated playlists or IPTV credentials
  • Sell the app or charge for access to it
  • Claim it as your own work
What if a rights holder wants to contact BurgTV about misuse?

We welcome direct communication from rights holders. For copyright issues, contact dmca@burgtv.com. For abuse reports, contact abuse@burgtv.com.

BurgTV does not and cannot identify specific users, as the app does not collect such data. However, we cooperate fully with law enforcement in response to valid legal process.

What jurisdiction governs BurgTV?

BurgTV is developed and operated from Switzerland. Our Terms of Service are governed by Swiss law. Our data handling follows the Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (nFADP) and, where applicable, GDPR (via Swiss–EU adequacy).

For EU users, we comply with EU consumer protection rights, including the right of withdrawal (14-day cooling-off period for Premium subscriptions).

Does BurgTV support two-way communication / chat / user-to-user content?

No. BurgTV is a media player. It does not offer any social features, chat, user-generated content hosting, messaging, or peer-to-peer sharing. This reduces our regulatory footprint and matches the definition of a "passive information society service" under EU e-commerce law.

Do you use my data for AI training or sell it to third parties?

No, absolutely not. BurgTV does not sell, rent, or lease user data to any third party. We do not use user data for AI training. The only data collected are anonymous crash reports (Firebase Crashlytics) used exclusively to diagnose and fix bugs.

How do I delete my account and all data?

You have the right to erasure under GDPR Article 17, CCPA, LGPD, and equivalent laws. Visit our Account Deletion page or email privacy@burgtv.com.

We fulfill deletion requests within 30 days (typically much sooner).
